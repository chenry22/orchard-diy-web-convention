What is a website?

When we think of a website, we usually just think of the final product. Whatever appears on our screen, that is the website. Most websites we visit are for... things... companies promoting themselves or a collection of information and resources or just something that has some goal beyond existing.

First, I think it's important to think of websites as a little more dimensional than just the final product. I think the most apt analogy I've seen is comparing web dev/design to architecture (click here for a fuller exploration of this idea). Most simply, a website is a place that people can visit, and a thing with layers of structure that create the final experience.

There are three basic building blocks of a website: HTML (defining the structure/essential content), CSS (defining the styling & aesthetics), and JavaScript (defining any ways in which the site is dynamic or interactive). There are a lot of resources which go into these three elements in great detail, and we encourage you to explore those if you're curious!

d d d

Back to the main question though: what is a website? Literally it is those three elements listed above (with varying amounts of extra layers from different web frameworks). But it can really be... anything. Websites can be art, they can be a form of play, they can be an archive, they can be a community center. I think it's important to think of websites in this way. When you look at any site, really consider, "What is the essence of this thing?"
